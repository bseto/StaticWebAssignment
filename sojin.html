History

Park Jae-beom, born April 25, 1987, is an American singer, songwriter, rapper, dancer, record producer, model, chroreographer, entrepreneur and actor.

Park Jae-beom is the founder and CEO of the independant hip hop record label AOMG.

Originally, he rose to fame as the leader of the South Korean boy band 2PM formed by JYP Entertainment in 2008. However, Jay Park returned home to Seattle after comments he wrote about Korea in 2005 as a teenager were publicized by the Korean Media.

Interesting Facts

When Jay Park returned home to Seattle, he appeared in some of Wong Fu Productions YouTube videos.

Jay Park uploaded a cover video of B.o.B's hit "Nothin' no You" with his own rap and lyrics to YouTube on March 2010. The video went viral and reached over 2,000,000 views in less than 24 hours, leading to the release of Jay Park's first EP, "Count on Me" in July 2010. The Album debuted at number one on the Gaon Chart.
